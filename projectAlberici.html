Alberici Corporate Headquarters

Location: St. Louis, Missouri, USA
Contract Size: $29,000,000 USD
Project Size: 110,000 sq. ft.
Start Date: September 2003
Completion Date: December 2004

Alberici Constructors, Inc. and Vertegy collaborated to convert a 50-year-old manufacturing facility into Class-A office space for their parent company, Alberici Corporation. The design of the project called for removing a 70-foot-wide, 485-foot-long section from the middle of the existing two-story warehouse. This virtually sliced the building into two parts. With an outdoor courtyard in the gap, one side was converted to covered parking to replace the heat-absorbing asphalt on the site and the other side was renovated into open, sun-lit office space. A few of the environmentally friendly features of the building include the following:

  • Recycling portions of the existing building that were demolished to eliminate landfilled materials
  • Addition of operable clerestories for ventilation and atriums for natural light, reducing the need for artificial light and improving employee connectivity to the outdoors
  • Raised floor construction in office areas, with floor vents for individually controlled warm and cool air distribution
  • Energy-efficient mechanical systems that reduce energy consumption
  • The creation of two ponds for stormwater management and restoration of habitat
  • Native prairie landscaping, which eliminates the need for permanent irrigation systems

After achieving LEED Platinum in 2005, the Alberici building still remains one of the highest rated LEED-NC buildings in the world and continues to host hundreds of visitors each year. The building fosters the inspiring, open and collaborative working environment as envisioned by the owner, while conserving energy, the environment and precious natural resources.

Services

The Vertegy team provided all LEED-related services including design and construction team integration, teaming charrettes, documentation, coordination and submission of LEED certification package, energy modeling, glass/glazing/lighting design consultation, fundamental and enhanced commissioning, and LEED process management with USGBC. Additionally, Thomas Taylor served as both the Owner's Representative and Project Director, overseeing the entire project from pre-design through construction completion and LEED certification.
